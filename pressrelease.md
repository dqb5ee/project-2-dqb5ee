# The NBA's Best-Kept Secret: High Performers, Low Paychecks

## Hook
In a league where star power commands $40M+ salaries, some of the most
productive players on the 2024–25 court are being paid like role players —
and most front offices haven't noticed yet.

## Problem Statement
NBA teams operate under a strict salary cap, meaning every roster dollar is
a strategic decision. But compensation is often driven by reputation and prior
contracts rather than current performance. In the 2024–25 season, several
players posting elite scoring and playmaking numbers are earning a fraction of
what their production would suggest they're worth, while others in the middle
tier are collecting top-tier paychecks. This misalignment between pay and
production represents a significant inefficiency that analytically-minded front
offices can exploit.

## Solution Description
Using a technique called k-means clustering, this analysis grouped all NBA
players from the 2024–25 season into three performance tiers — High, Medium,
and Low — based purely on their total points and assists. Salary was then
overlaid to identify players whose pay does not match their tier.

The result is a clear, visual shortlist of "Hidden Gems": players delivering
top-tier production at a below-market price. Our analysis identified a specific
group of high-performance athletes — led by **Jalen Duren**, **Alperen Şengün**,
and **Zach Edey** — who provide elite value relative to their rookie-scale or
value-contract salaries. These are the ideal targets for any team looking to
maximize output within strict salary cap constraints.

## What This Means
The data is clear: elite production does not always come with an elite price tag.
In a league defined by the zero-sum reality of the salary cap, the teams that
win championships are rarely the ones who spend the most — they are the ones who
spend the smartest. The players identified in this analysis represent a genuine
market inefficiency, a window that closes the moment a player's next contract
reflects their true value. For any front office willing to let the data lead,
the 2025 offseason offers a rare opportunity to build a more competitive roster
without breaking the bank. The value gap is real, and it is available right now
to any team paying attention.
